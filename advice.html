Advice & Tips for Femboys

Whether you're just starting to explore or have been embracing your identity for a while, here are some helpful and practical tips:

.

Confidence & Self-Expression

Stepping into your authentic self can be intimidating, especially in public spaces- but you're not alone. Start small: explore your style in safe, affirming environments or online communities. With time and self-kindness, your confidence will grow. You deserve to take up space, be seen, and be celebrated just as you are.

Whether you're into soft pastels, playful skirts, or a more androgynous, minimalist vibe- there’s no single way to look or feel like a femboy. Shaving & Hair Removal

Hair removal can be part of your gender expression or just a personal preference- there’s no right or wrong way to do it. For shaving, always use a sharp, clean razor to reduce irritation and avoid cuts. Shave after a warm shower when your pores are open and your hair is softened. Use a gentle, unscented shaving cream or gel, and shave in the direction of hair growth to minimize razor burn and ingrown hairs.

Legs & Arms: These areas are generally easier to shave. Use long, smooth strokes and rinse the blade often. Exfoliate 1–2 times a week to prevent ingrowns and keep skin smooth. After shaving, apply a fragrance-free moisturizer or aloe-based lotion.

Chest: Chest skin is often more sensitive. Shave gently and avoid going over the same spot repeatedly. If you’re prone to irritation, try trimming instead of shaving, or consider waxing or epilation for longer-lasting results. Always moisturize afterward to soothe the skin.

If you want longer-lasting results across any area, look into waxing, epilators (which pull hair from the root), or professional treatments like laser hair removal. Test on a small area first to see how your skin reacts. Be kind to your skin, and make your routine something that feels good- not just something that looks good.

Skin Care Basics

A good skincare routine doesn’t have to be complicated or expensive- consistency is what truly makes the difference. At the core, aim for these basics:

  • Cleanser: Use a gentle facial cleanser every morning and night to remove dirt, oil, and makeup without stripping your skin.
  • Toner (optional): A toner can help balance your skin’s pH and prep it for moisturizer, but it’s not a must-have for everyone.
  • Moisturizer: Apply a hydrating, fragrance-free moisturizer after cleansing to keep your skin soft and protected- yes, even if your skin is oily.
  • Sunscreen: Use a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher every morning, even on cloudy days. Sun protection is essential for healthy, youthful skin.

Everyone's skin is different- so if you're dealing with acne, sensitivity, or specific concerns, consider consulting a dermatologist. The goal isn’t perfection; it’s taking care of yourself in a way that feels good.

Hair Styling & Maintenance

Hair care should suit your texture, length, and personal style. Whether your hair is straight, wavy, curly, or coily, a consistent routine will help it look and feel its best. Start with gentle shampooing two to three times a week to avoid stripping natural oils. Always follow with a conditioner that suits your hair type.

Straight Hair: This hair type gets oily faster, so lightweight shampoos and conditioners work best. Avoid heavy styling products. Use a volumizing spray or dry shampoo between washes to maintain body. Trim regularly to prevent limp or split ends.

Wavy Hair: Wavy hair benefits from moisturizing shampoos and conditioners. Let your hair air-dry when possible to enhance your natural wave pattern. A light mousse or wave-enhancing cream can add texture without crunchiness.

Curly Hair: Use sulfate-free, moisturizing products. Curly hair tends to be drier and more prone to frizz. After washing, apply a curl cream or leave-in conditioner and let it air-dry or use a diffuser on low heat. Avoid brushing when dry. Instead, detangle gently in the shower with a wide-tooth comb while your hair is wet and coated with conditioner.

Coily/Kinky Hair: This texture needs deep moisture. Wash with gentle, hydrating shampoos once a week, and co-wash (use conditioner only) between washes if needed. Deep condition regularly and use leave-in products and oils like argan or jojoba to lock in moisture. Protective styles like twists, braids, or buns can help reduce breakage and maintain length.

Regardless of type, avoid frequent heat styling and always use heat protectant when you do. Letting your hair air-dry when possible is better for its health. Get regular trims every 6 to 10 weeks.

For a more feminine or soft appearance, consider styles like curtain bangs, long layers, or gentle face-framing cuts. Short hair can still be styled with texture or asymmetry for an expressive look. Long hair offers versatility for buns, braids, or soft waves. Find a look that makes you feel affirmed and comfortable.

Voice Training & Communication

Voice training is an essential part of self-expression. Whether you want a softer, more feminine voice or a more androgynous tone, practice and consistency are key. There are many resources available online, including apps and YouTube channels, that can help guide you through exercises to improve pitch, resonance, and intonation.

1. Practice Daily: Voice training, like any skill, requires consistent practice. Set aside time each day to work on your voice, focusing on pitch, breath control, and clarity. Over time, you’ll notice improvements.

2. Work with a Professional: Consider working with a speech therapist or voice coach, especially if you’re looking to make significant changes to your voice. They can provide personalized guidance and feedback.

3. Experiment with Intonation: Play around with the way you speak. Experimenting with tone, pitch, and rhythm can help you develop a voice that feels authentic and comfortable for you.

4. Take Care of Your Voice: Don’t strain your voice during practice. Stay hydrated, rest your vocal cords, and avoid excessive shouting or whispering. The more care you give your voice, the better it will sound over time.

Dealing with Negativity or Online Harassment

Unfortunately, the internet can sometimes be a space where negativity or harassment happens. Whether it's trolling, hate speech, or personal attacks, it’s important to remember that these behaviors reflect the insecurities of others, not your worth.

1. Trust Your Instincts: If someone is making you feel unsafe, uncomfortable, or targeted, your feelings are valid. It's okay to distance yourself from people or spaces that don't respect you.

2. Block, Mute, and Report: Social media platforms, Discord, and other online spaces often have tools to protect you. Don't hesitate to use the block, mute, or report features when someone crosses the line. Blocking someone isn’t a sign of weakness—it's a step toward protecting your mental health and peace.

3. Set Clear Boundaries: If someone is pushing your boundaries, it’s okay to assert yourself and communicate your limits. You can say things like, "Please don’t speak to me this way," or, "I’m not comfortable with this conversation." You have the right to expect respect.

4. Don’t Engage with Trolls: Online trolls thrive on attention. Responding can escalate the situation. Often, it's best to disengage and report the behavior instead. Don’t feel pressured to justify yourself or your identity to others.

5. Seek Support: Reach out to your online friends or community members for support if you feel overwhelmed. Surround yourself with people who understand and respect you. Sometimes just talking about your experience can help you process your feelings.

6. Prioritize Your Mental Health: Online harassment can affect your well-being. Take breaks from social media or take time away from negative spaces if needed. Practice self-care, whether that's stepping outside for a walk, journaling, or engaging in hobbies that make you feel good.

Remember: Online negativity and harassment don’t define who you are. You deserve to be in spaces where you're respected, celebrated, and supported.

Mental Health Support

It’s completely normal to feel overwhelmed or stressed from time to time, especially when navigating identity, online spaces, or personal challenges. Remember, seeking support is a sign of strength, not weakness. You don't have to face everything alone.

1. Reach Out to Friends: Talking to someone you trust, whether a friend, family member, or online community member, can help lighten the emotional load. Sometimes, just sharing how you feel can be a huge relief.

2. Consider Professional Help: Speaking with a counselor or therapist can provide invaluable support, offering tools for coping and helping you navigate tough emotions. If therapy feels daunting or out of reach, apps like BetterHelp or Talkspace offer online therapy with licensed professionals.

3. Try Mental Health Apps: There are various apps designed to support mental health. Apps like Calm, Headspace, and Insight Timer offer guided meditations, relaxation exercises, and sleep aids. They can help reduce anxiety, manage stress, and improve your overall well-being.

4. Practice Self-Care: Take time for activities that nourish you, whether that’s going for a walk, taking a relaxing bath, journaling, or simply resting. Prioritize activities that bring you peace and recharge your energy.

5. Find Communities of Support: Online communities can be a great place to find people who understand what you're going through. Seek spaces where mental health is prioritized, and where members are empathetic and encouraging.

Your mental health matters, and you deserve to take care of it just as much as you would your physical health. Don’t hesitate to ask for help when you need it—there’s no shame in prioritizing your well-being.

Experimenting with Identity

Your identity is unique to you, and it is completely normal to explore and experiment as you figure out what feels right. You may try different names, pronouns, or appearances as a way to better express yourself. There is no pressure to have everything figured out right away. Identity is a fluid and evolving journey.

1. Try Different Names and Pronouns: Experimenting with different names or pronouns can be a powerful way to connect more deeply with yourself. You do not need to commit to anything right away. Try things out in safe spaces or with people you trust to see how they feel.

2. Play with Appearance: Explore different looks that align with how you feel. Whether it is fashion, makeup, hairstyle, or even body modifications, allow yourself to play around with different aesthetics. You can change things based on your mood or season of life. There is no one “right” way to look.

3. Embrace Fluidity: Your identity may evolve over time, and that is okay. What you feel today might not be how you feel tomorrow. Do not feel like you need to have a “final” version of your identity. Embrace the fluidity and let it unfold naturally.

4. Be Kind to Yourself: Identity exploration can be challenging, especially if you feel pressure from others to define yourself. Take it slow and give yourself grace as you experiment and discover what feels authentic. You are worthy of love and respect regardless of where you are in your journey.

Remember, your identity is yours to shape, and you have the freedom to change it as you grow. There is no rush or pressure to conform to anyone’s expectations. What matters most is what makes *you* feel good.

Building a Supportive Network

A strong, supportive network is crucial to your well-being. Surround yourself with people who uplift, encourage, and accept you for who you are. Having a community to turn to when you need advice or reassurance can make a big difference.

1. Find Like-Minded Individuals: Seek out online and offline communities that align with your values. Whether it’s a Discord server, a social media group, or a local support group, find people who understand and accept you.

2. Be Open About Your Needs: Let your friends and loved ones know what kind of support you need. Open communication is the foundation of any strong relationship, so don’t be afraid to express your feelings.

3. Offer Support in Return: Building a supportive network is a two-way street. Be there for others, offer encouragement, and create a safe, accepting space for your friends and peers.

4. Seek Out Mentorship: Find mentors who can offer guidance and advice on your journey. This could be someone who has gone through similar experiences or someone who can provide perspective and wisdom.

Finding Inspiration

Inspiration can come from many places. Follow creators, femboys, and individuals who inspire you online. Their stories, styles, and energy can offer fresh ideas and help you feel more connected to your own journey. Whether it’s fashion, confidence, or self-expression, seeing others share their truth can empower you to do the same.

1. Learn from Their Style: Observe how different creators express themselves through clothing, makeup, and overall aesthetic. Don’t be afraid to experiment and adapt elements that resonate with you. Your style is an extension of your identity, so let it be as unique as you are.

2. Connect with Their Stories: Many creators share personal stories about their journeys. Their struggles, triumphs, and experiences can help you feel less alone and more empowered to embrace your own path. You might find common ground or be inspired by how they overcame challenges.

3. Absorb Their Energy: Pay attention to the confidence and positivity they project. Many creators exude a sense of self-assurance that can be contagious. Let their energy motivate you to embrace who you are, unapologetically.

Ready to start? Check out our curated social media list for a collection of inspiring communities. Follow those who speak to you, and take your time discovering new voices and perspectives that enrich your experience.

Budget-Friendly Fashion & Makeup

You don’t need to spend a lot of money to look and feel great. There are plenty of budget-friendly options for fashion and makeup that will allow you to experiment with different looks without breaking the bank.

1. Thrift Stores & Secondhand Shops: Thrift stores are a great place to find unique and affordable clothing. Many secondhand items are in great condition and can be customized to suit your style.

2. Drugstore Makeup: Drugstore brands offer affordable, high-quality makeup options that are perfect for experimenting with different looks. Look for versatile products that can be used for multiple purposes.

3. DIY Customization: Consider modifying or altering existing clothing to fit your personal style. Adding patches, lace, or other decorative elements can turn a simple piece of clothing into something special.

4. Look for Sales and Discounts: Many fashion brands and makeup retailers have sales or discounts throughout the year. Take advantage of these to stock up on the essentials or to treat yourself to something new.
